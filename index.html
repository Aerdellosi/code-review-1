  • The Reflecting Pool

    tl;dr: A forum where users can discuss original insights (o.I.0 -- original insights only) within the format of:

    1. A brief anecdote of how the insight was derived.
    2. The insight put into a standard-form argument.
    3. A detailed list of collected evidence both in support of and against the posited argument.

    Other users will discuss the validity and soundness of the argument in the comments section below.

    Other sub-forums will include topic such as:

    • Journaling
    • Meditation
    • Lucid Dreaming
    • Journaling
    • Breathing
    • Altered Consciousness
    • Logic

    As well as two meta sections: Ban Appeals, and 'the Mollusk Trench', a place which acts as a general discussion with minimal rules.

    Languages used: Javascript, HTML, CSS, C#

    Longer description:

    In the dusty alleyways of ancient Athens a cobbler stoops over a boiling cauldron in his steamy shop, softening the leather for his sandals. A girl comes in, about the age of seven, and walks up to the cobbler. He pretends not to notice. She pulls at the edge of his peplos, and he motions to the rack by the door. She nods, pulls out two drachms, and hands it the cobbler. As the cobbler turns around and reaches for the coins, he pauses. Insight flashes in his mind. He smiles broadly, grabs the coins, escorts the girl out the door, finishes his boil, places his leather out to mold, closes his shop and then hikes to the Agora. It's at the agora that he'll discuss his ideas with the city's philosophers and evaluate them through dialectic for their validity and soundness.

    This process is often mirrored in online discourse; someone moves through their life, thinks of something interesting, and then shares it on social media with the intent of allowing their network to evaluate it....But the user runs into a problem: In virtually all serious or semi-serious discourse online, other users will dismiss their argument outright unless either a piece of it or its entirety originates from someone who's established themselves as having intellectual clout, either an expert, or a philosopher; instead of tweeting about how important it is to plant hardwood trees, the user has to cite Marcus Aurelius saying the exact same thing.

    In other words, unless a man in the 21st century cites a man from two thousand years ago, his argument gets ignored irrespective of its value.

    Under the premise that 'if it truly is timeless, timeless wisdom will resurface given enough time', the Reflecting Pool offers an explicitly structured platform of discourse where users can explore both their o.I.0 arguments as well as their methods and techniques of derivation similar to the Agora of ancient of Athens.
